SAYING NO
  • "Let me think about it and get back to you" is a complete answer.
  • One reason maximum. Zero is allowed.
  • Explaining more is not persuading more. It is negotiating against yourself.
  • Say it with the shake. The shake is not visible from outside.
  • Their anger is not evidence you are wrong. It is evidence they wanted a yes.
  • The silence after your no is their turn. Do not fill it.

  • "I am not able to do that."

FACT vs FEELING
"Work was busy""I felt useless at work today."
"The trip was fine""I felt close to you on that trip."
"Traffic was bad""I felt cornered all day."
"The meeting ran long""I felt dismissed in that meeting."
"I am tired""I felt defeated today."
If the sentence works without the word "I felt," it is a fact. Facts do not build closeness.

THE BASICS

What is A and V?
A is Assert: you said no, held a boundary, asked for something. V is Vulnerable: you shared a feeling or admitted something. Every rep is one or the other.
Why rate distress 0 to 10 twice?
It is the same question before and after: distress right now. The drop between the two ratings is the point. After 20 reps the Review tab shows it in your own numbers.
What is the prediction check?
Before, you tap the thing you are afraid will happen. After, you tap what actually happened. Reality is almost always milder than the prediction, and Review shows you that gap.
How do I log the fastest way?
Before: tap A or V, a distress number, a fear. Go do the thing. After: a distress number, what happened, and one of HELD / PARTIAL / FOLDED. The outcome tap saves. Feeling and notes are optional.
What is a "pending" rep?
If you finish the "before" step and close the app, the rep waits. It shows on the home screen and jumps straight to the "after" step on reopen. Nothing is lost. You can also discard it from the after screen.
What is the TODAY card?
One suggested rep, rotated daily, so you never face a blank choice. Tap it to start, or tap the x to skip it for today. Skipping costs nothing.
What is the anchor?
A routine you already do, with one rep attached: "after my morning coffee, one Tier 1 rep." Tying a rep to an existing habit is the strongest follow-through trick there is. Tap the line under the buttons to set or change it.

THE LADDER

Are tiers locked?
No. Every tier is open from day one. The lower tiers are the easiest place to build the skill, so lean on them early, but a real win in any tier always counts.
The three outcome buttons?
For an assert rep: HELD you did it and stuck with it, PARTIAL you did some, FOLDED you caved. For a vulnerable rep they read SAID IT / PARTIAL / STAYED QUIET. Either way, caving still counts as a rep. The folds are the data, not a failure.

YOUR DATA

Backup vs Restore?
Backup saves an encrypted file of your reps (in Tools). Restore reads one back in. Back up now and then. Restore only when moving to a new device or after erasing.
If I lock and unlock, do I lose anything?
No. Same passphrase opens the same data. Locking is just the daily normal.
How do I delete everything?
Lock the app, then tap "Erase everything" on the unlock screen and type ERASE. This is permanent. It is not a passphrase reset.
I logged something by mistake. Undo?
Right after saving, tap Undo in the confirmation that pops up. For older ones, go to the History list on the Review tab and tap the \u00d7 on that row, then tap it again to confirm.
Can I use it on my laptop and phone both?
Not synced live, but you can keep one combined log. Log on either device, then use Merge backups to fold them together without losing anything. Export a fresh backup of the merged result and restore it onto the other device so both match. Same passphrase on all of them.
Do reps get a date and time?
Yes, automatically, the moment you save. You see the date in History, and the CSV export has separate date and time columns.
What if I forget the passphrase?
The data is gone, on the device and in every backup. That is the trade for nobody else being able to read it. Keep the four words on paper.
Can I share the app itself?
Yes. The link only serves the empty app; anyone opening it sets their own passphrase and gets their own private log. What you must not share are the backup file and especially the CSV. Those are the actual data.
